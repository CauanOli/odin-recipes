Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a stew of beans with beef and pork. The name feijoada is derived from feijão, 'bean' in Portuguese. It is widely prepared in the Portuguese-speaking world, with slight variations. The basic ingredients of feijoada are beans and fresh pork or beef. In Brazil, it is usually made with black beans. The stew is best prepared over low heat in a thick clay pot.

Ingredients

Toasted Manioc Flour

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, very thinly sliced
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 1/2 cups manioc flour
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped flat-leaf parsley
  • Kosher salt

Feijoada

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 pounds dried black beans, soaked overnight and drained
  • 3 1/2 quarts water
  • 1 pound fresh spicy sausage, such as linguiça
  • 3/4 pound dried beef (carne seca) or corned beef, in one piece
  • 1 1/2 pounds smoked pork chops
  • 3/4 pound lean slab bacon
  • 3/4 pound chorizo, in one piece
  • 1 dried red chile
  • Kosher salt

Steps

Make the Toasted Manioc Flour

  1. Melt the butter in a large skillet.
  2. Add the onion and cook over low heat until softened, about 7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the egg and cook, stirring, until dry and crumbly, about 30 seconds.
  4. Add the manioc flour and cook over moderate heat, stirring occasionally, until lightly toasted, about 8 minutes.
  5. Stir in the parsley and season with salt.

Make the Feijoada

Step 1

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy casserole.
  2. Add the garlic and onion and cook over moderately low heat, stirring occasionally, until the onion is softened, about 7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the drained black beans.
  4. Add the water to the casserole and bring to a boil over moderately high heat.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the beans for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add all of the meats and the dried chile and cook until the beans are tender, about 1 hour longer.

Step 2

  1. Remove the meats from the casserole and thickly slice them; discard any bones.
  2. Pick out and discard the chile.
  3. Season the beans with salt.
  4. Ladle the beans into shallow bowls and serve with the sliced meats.
  5. Pass the toasted manioc flour at the table for sprinkling over the feijoada.
